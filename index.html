About the Society

Nomash Electrical Engineering Fellows Society connects students with careers, practical skills, and community.

Our Mission

The Nomash Electrical Engineering Fellows Society (NEFS) is a student-run society for electrical engineering students and anyone interested in electrical, electronic, software, systems, or technology-related fields. We help students connect classroom learning with real professional practice through career events, hands-on workshops, social activities, and innovation challenges.

NEFS is organized by students and supported by the Department of Electrical Engineering. Membership is free and open to all students who want to learn, meet peers, explore industry pathways, or take part in friendly engineering activities.

History

Founded in 2019 by a small group of undergraduate electrical engineering students, NEFS began as a weekly study group in the engineering library. By 2022, the society had grown into a larger student community and started hosting industry networking nights. In 2024, NEFS launched Innovation Challenge Day, now one of its flagship events for collaborative problem solving.

What We Offer

Career Events

Industry nights, graduate pathways panels, resume clinics, and informal Q&A sessions with engineers, alumni, and early-career professionals.

Workshops

Beginner-friendly project workshops where students practise technical skills, build confidence, and learn through small hands-on activities.

Social Events

Meet-and-connect lunches, casual gatherings, and peer networking opportunities that help students feel part of the engineering community.

Innovation Challenges

Team-based design days where students solve a small engineering or technology problem and present their ideas in a supportive setting.
